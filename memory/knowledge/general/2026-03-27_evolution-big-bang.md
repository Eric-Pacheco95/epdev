## Key Findings
- The Big Bang is an expansion of space itself (not an explosion in space) from an initial singularity 13.8 billion years ago; four independent lines of evidence confirm it: Hubble redshift, CMB (2.7K afterglow), Big Bang nucleosynthesis (predicted H/He/Li ratios match observations), and large-scale structure
- Heavy elements beyond hydrogen and helium were forged in prior generations of stars via stellar nucleosynthesis — everything heavier than iron comes from neutron capture in supernovae and neutron star mergers; "we are literally stardust"
- Leading abiogenesis hypotheses: RNA World (RNA as both genetic material and catalyst), hydrothermal vents (energy gradients + mineral catalysts), and terrestrial hot springs (wet-dry cycles concentrate organics); current consensus favors a combination, likely RNA-like molecules in hydrothermal environments
- Evolution by natural selection has four components: variation, inheritance, differential survival, differential reproduction; additional mechanisms include genetic drift, sexual selection, horizontal gene transfer, epigenetics, and evo-devo
- The Cambrian Explosion (~540 Mya) produced most modern animal phyla within ~20 million years — one of the most dramatic evolutionary accelerations in the fossil record

## Context
This is a broad scientific literacy brief covering two foundational pillars of modern science. The solar system formed ~4.6 billion years ago from enriched stellar debris (9.2 billion years after the Big Bang). The Great Oxygenation Event (~2.4 Bya), triggered by cyanobacterial photosynthesis, transformed Earth's atmosphere and enabled complex aerobic life. Eukaryotic cells emerged ~2.0 Bya via endosymbiosis.
