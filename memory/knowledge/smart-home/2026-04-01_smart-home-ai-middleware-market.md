## Key Findings
- Smart home market: $52.65B (2024) → $66.45B (2029) at 4.8% CAGR (conservative); hardware is 54.6% of the market; consumer willingness to pay for intelligence middleware is critically suppressed because Google, Amazon, and Apple bundle AI free (Alexa+ is free for Prime subscribers)
- **Consumer play is risky**: All three platform giants racing to add AI-driven proactive intelligence to their ecosystems in 2026 — standalone intelligence middleware is a very hard consumer sell; Apple-Google partnership means Gemini powers both ecosystems
- **Strongest solo-builder signal**: B2B short-term rental/property management niche — operators already pay $50–200/month for AI tools and report 20–35% ROI improvements; structural moat exists before scale matters
- **Home Assistant ecosystem** (2M+ installations, 56 full-time staff, $6.50/mo cloud, 70K+ contributors): no paid add-on marketplace — HACS integrations are free/community-built; revenue model is cloud services + hardware, not intelligence layers; building paid integrations here faces community resistance
- IoT security monitoring is a legitimate niche — Firewalla's hardware+software model ($350–500 one-time) shows willingness to pay; pure-software equivalent needs a different revenue model

## Context
The research applies the "don't build" filter from CLAUDE.md: (1) Is AI/intelligence being bundled free by incumbents? Yes — Amazon Alexa+ free for Prime. (2) Are there structural moats before scale matters? Yes in B2B property management, not in consumer. (3) Does WTP survive bundling pressure? Not in consumer; yes in B2B operators. The IoT middleware market is $23.57B (2026) → $58.63B (2032) but this is enterprise-wide, not home-specific.
