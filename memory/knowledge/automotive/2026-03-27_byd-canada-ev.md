## Key Findings
- BYD is entering Canada following a January 2026 trade deal cutting Chinese EV tariffs from 100% to 6.1%; 49,000 units allowed in year one; demo units expected mid-2026, retail late 2026 in GTA/Vancouver/Calgary/Montreal
- **BYD Seal is Eric's best fit** — BMW 3-Series size class (4,800mm), 460–570km WLTP range, 0-100km/h in 3.8s (AWD), Blade LFP battery, rotating 15.6" touchscreen, Apple CarPlay, V2L; est. $45K–$60K CAD
- **Critical disadvantage**: Chinese-built EVs are explicitly excluded from Canada's $5,000 federal EVAP rebate (requires Canadian manufacture or FTA country origin; China has no FTA with Canada); Hyundai Ioniq 6 and Tesla Model 3 both qualify
- The $5K rebate gap brings the Ioniq 6 to ~$41.8K effective price — BYD must price the Seal at $42K–$44K to remain competitive; if it lands at $50K+ the Ioniq 6 wins on value
- Ontario has no provincial EV rebate in 2026; Quebec offers up to $2,000 (Roulez vert, <$65K MSRP) — BYD may qualify if the vehicle makes the eligible list before the program ends Dec 31, 2026

## Context
BYD is the world's largest EV maker (2.26M battery-electric vehicles in 2025, surpassing Tesla at 1.64M). The Ioniq 6 currently wins at ~$41.8K effective price given the rebate advantage. BYD's Seal only wins outright if its Canadian price comes in below ~$43K CAD, which is possible but not confirmed. Official Canadian pricing has not been announced.

## Open Questions
- Will BYD price the Seal aggressively enough to compensate for the $5K rebate disadvantage?
- Will Quebec's Roulez vert program accept BYD vehicles before the Dec 2026 deadline?
- Does the Blade LFP battery's cold-weather performance gap versus NMC matter for Toronto winters?
