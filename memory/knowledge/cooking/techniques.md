# Cooking Techniques — The WHY Behind Each Method

> Method fluency is the goal. Each technique unlocks a family of dishes.

## Pilaf (rice method)

**The move**: bloom aromatics + spices in fat → toast dry rice in the fat → add liquid → cover/simmer → rest.

**Why it works**: fat carries fat-soluble flavor compounds from spices into every grain; toasting coats rice in fat, preventing clumping and adding nutty depth; resting finishes the steam uniformly.

**Ratio baseline**: 1 cup rice : 1.5-2 cups liquid (varies by rice type). ½ tsp salt per cup dry rice. 1-2 tbsp fat.

**Works with**: any whole-spice or ground-spice blend, any fat (butter, oil, ghee), any liquid (water, broth, coconut milk).

**Common failure**: not rinsing rice → gluey; not resting → uneven texture; browning garlic → bitter.

## Spice Blooming

**The move**: warm whole or ground spices in fat until fragrant (30-90 sec), then add other ingredients.

**Why it works**: many spice aromatic compounds are fat-soluble and heat-activated — raw spices taste muted/harsh; bloomed spices taste round and deep. This is the single highest-leverage technique in most world cuisines.

**Temperature**: medium to medium-low. Too hot = burn (especially ground spices like paprika, turmeric). Whole spices (cumin seed, cardamom, clove) tolerate more heat.

**Order**: aromatics (garlic/onion) first → whole spices → ground spices last (they burn fastest).

## Sear-Rest (protein method)

**The move**: dry protein surface → hot pan + fat → don't move it → flip once → finish → rest 5-10 min off heat.

**Why it works**: Maillard browning requires dry surface + high heat; moving protein prevents crust formation; resting lets muscle fibers reabsorb juices that would otherwise pour out on cut.

**Rest is non-negotiable**: cutting hot = dry meat. Tent loosely with foil.

## Pan Sauce (from the fond)

**The move**: after searing protein, remove it to rest → deglaze pan with liquid (wine, stock, juice) → scrape fond → reduce → mount with butter off-heat → pour over rested protein.

**Why it works**: "fond" (browned bits stuck to pan) is concentrated flavor; deglazing dissolves it into sauce. Mounting with cold butter at the end emulsifies for glossy texture.

**Skip if**: pan was non-stick (no fond forms) or burnt (bitter sauce).

## Aromatics in Fat (universal opener)

**The move**: heat fat in pan → add aromatics (garlic, onion, ginger, shallot) → sauté until fragrant/soft but not browned.

**Why it works**: this is the first step of nearly every world cuisine. Fat extracts and carries aromatic compounds; slow cook (not brown) = sweet depth; browned/burnt = bitter.

**Temperature rule**: garlic burns at high heat in 30 sec → always add garlic AFTER onion has softened, or cook both at lower heat.
