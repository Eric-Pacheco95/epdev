# Fintech Domain Knowledge

Last updated: 2026-04-15

## Domain Overview

Fintech and banking AI adoption is in a late-pilot / early-production phase globally. Canadian market is a distinct sub-context: regulatory pressure (OSFI E-23), concentrated Big 5 spend, and a documented consulting gap between strategy-only Big 4 firms and execution-capable specialists. Global spend is large but ROI realization remains thin.

## Key Findings

### Canadian Banking AI Consulting
- Big 5 annual tech spend: RBC C$5B+, TD C$4B+; RBC projects C$700M-$1B AI value by 2027, TD targets C$1B.
- OSFI Guideline E-23 (Model Risk Management, effective May 1 2027) mandates enterprise-wide AI/ML governance for all FRFIs; third-party black-box model governance is flagged as the hardest compliance gap.
- Consulting gap: Big 4 sell strategy decks at $300-600/hr but lack execution depth; creates opening for implementation-focused specialists.

### Global Banking AI Adoption
- Global banking AI spend: $73B in 2025 (+17% YoY), projected $143-190B by 2030.
- 95% of GenAI initiatives remain in pilot stage; only 4 of the 50 largest banks show documented realized ROI.
- Fraud detection is the dominant deployed use case (90% of FIs); JPMorgan reported $1.5B in documented savings.
- Agentic AI is the next frontier but is blocked by explainability/auditability requirements at regulated institutions.
- Vendor landscape: hyperscalers (MS/Google/AWS) dominate large banks; Temenos/nCino/Backbase/Zest AI own mid-market.

## Cross-Cutting Themes
- Regulatory compliance (OSFI E-23, explainability requirements) is the primary near-term demand driver -- not productivity.
- ROI realization gap is wide: massive spend, thin confirmed returns; pilot-to-production conversion is the bottleneck.
- Agentic AI adoption is structurally gated by explainability, not just maturity -- this is a durable constraint, not a temporary lag.
- Canadian market may be an outlier on AI maturity rankings but that claim lacks cited methodology.
