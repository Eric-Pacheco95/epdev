## Key Findings

- Global banking AI spend: $73B in 2025 (+17% YoY) -> $143-190B by 2030; 95% of GenAI initiatives still in pilot, only 4 of 50 largest banks show realized ROI
- Fraud detection is the most deployed use case (90% of FIs); JPMorgan $1.5B documented savings; agentic AI is next but blocked by explainability requirements
- Vendor landscape: Hyperscalers (MS/Google/AWS) own large banks; Temenos/nCino/Backbase/Zest AI own mid-market; all released major AI platform updates April-May 2025
- Consulting rates: Big 4 at $300-600/hr (strategy), mid-tier $150-300/hr; specialist premium 30-40%; EY AI revenue up 30% in FY2025; practitioner/implementation tier is the gap
- EU AI Act August 2026 + OSFI E-23 May 2027 are the two regulatory guns-to-the-head creating non-discretionary consulting demand

## Context

Builds on prior 2026-04-03 article covering Canadian banking specifically (OSFI E-23, Big 5 spend, employment constraints).
