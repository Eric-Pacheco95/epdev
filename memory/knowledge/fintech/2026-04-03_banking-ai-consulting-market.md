## Key Findings
- Canadian Big 5 banks spend C$5B+ (RBC) to C$4B (TD) annually on technology; RBC expects $700M-$1B AI value by 2027; TD targets $1B; Canada ranked #1 globally for AI maturity in banking
- OSFI Guideline E-23 (Model Risk Management) effective May 1, 2027 creates compliance-driven demand — all FRFIs must have enterprise-wide AI/ML governance frameworks; third-party "black box" model governance is the hardest part
- Consulting landscape gap: Big 4 sell strategy decks ($300-600/hr), mid-tier sells Copilot deploys ($150-300/hr), nobody sells hands-on banking automation implementation at the practitioner level — especially not for credit unions/small FIs
- Big 5 bank employment agreements universally require disclosure and approval for outside business; consulting for competing FIs would be blocked; adjacent markets (credit unions, fintechs, insurance, education) are lower-conflict paths
- Open banking (Consumer-Driven Banking Act) launching 2026 with API Read Access; Write Access in 2027 with RTR — creates time-bounded demand for people who understand both banking operations and technical implementation

## Context
Triggered by /extract-alpha identifying "bank automation knowledge + AI fluency = consultancy nobody in Canadian banking offers." The opportunity is real but employment-gated. The highest-signal entry is internal (pitch AI at own bank) or education-first (courses/templates on AI for regulated industries). OSFI E-23 May 2027 deadline is the strongest external demand driver.
